Higher-Order Iterate Lyapunov Exponents Tk

λk = k λ  ·  exact in the infinite-orbit limit

Generalising the single-step and double-step Lyapunov exponents to the k-fold iterate Tk of the Syracuse return map. Transfer-operator derivation, empirical mod-243 values, and a live interactive widget.

Section 1

Definition

For the k-fold iterate the Lyapunov exponent along an orbit is defined as the long-run average of the log-derivative of Tk:

λk = limN→∞ (1/N) Σm=0N−1 log |(Tk)′(nm)| where   nm+1 = Tk(nm)

By the chain rule the derivative of the composed map factorises as a product of single-step derivatives evaluated along the intermediate orbit:

(Tk)′(n) = T′(Tk−1(n)) · T′(Tk−2(n)) ··· T′(n)

Taking logarithms converts the product to a sum:

log |(Tk)′(n)| = Σj=0k−1 log |T′(Tj(n))|

The long-run average of a telescoping sum over the single-step log-derivatives recovers exactly k copies of the single-step exponent λ:

Exact result (infinite-orbit limit)

λk = k λ

The factor k is exact in the infinite-orbit limit. Finite-sample corrections arise only from short-range 2-adic correlations and vanish as N → ∞.

Section 2

Transfer-Operator Derivation

Let ℒ be the transfer operator for the single map T. For the k-fold iterate the transfer operator is simply ℒk. Spectral radius satisfies the multiplicative law:

ρ(ℒk) = [ρ(ℒ)]k

The Lyapunov exponent is the logarithm of the dominant eigenvalue, so the scaling is immediate:

λk = log ρ(ℒk) = k log ρ(ℒ) = k λ

Finite-resolution approximation PM

The finite modulus-2M transfer matrix PM has spectral radius ρ(PM) = 1/2M−1. Raising to the k-th power:

ρ(PMk) = (1/2M−1)k = 1/2k(M−1)

The finite-resolution Lyapunov exponent is therefore:

Finite-resolution formula

λk(M) = log ρ(PMk) = −k(M−1) log 2

When the modulus doubles (M → M+1), λk becomes k times more negative. The contraction accelerates linearly with the iterate order.
