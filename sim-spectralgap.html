Definition. For the k-fold transfer operator Lᵏ acting on a function space, the spectral gap is

  gap_k = ρ(Lᵏ) − |λ₂^(k)| = ρᵏ − |λ₂|ᵏ

where ρ = ρ(L) is the Perron–Frobenius leading eigenvalue and λ₂ is the second-largest in modulus.
Scaling law. Since |λ₂/ρ| = r < 1, we have

  gap_k = ρᵏ (1 − rᵏ) → ρᵏ  exponentially fast in k.

Every additional iterate multiplies both the leading eigenvalue and the gap by ρ, while the correction term rᵏ → 0 double-exponentially.
Finite-matrix case (mod 2^M). On the 2^(M−1) odd residues mod 2^M,

  ρ(P_M) = 1 / 2^(M−1)  and  all other eigenvalues = 0.

Therefore: gap_k^(M) = ρ(P_M^k) − 0 = 1 / 2^(k(M−1)).

The gap is maximal: the matrix is a scaled permutation matrix and the spectral gap equals the entire leading eigenvalue.
Lyapunov exponent. λ_k = k × λ where λ ≈ −0.286288 (log-average contraction per step of the Syracuse map). The negative Lyapunov exponent and the positive spectral gap together give double-exponential control: orbits contract in k while correlations decay in k.

Seed sentence (CEFR A2): "After more steps the map forgets the starting number faster and faster. The spectral gap grows with each extra step."
