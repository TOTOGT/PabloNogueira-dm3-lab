## 1. Definition of the Canonical Frequency Envelope

### 1.1 Littlewood‚ÄďPaley projectors

Fix a Schwartz function $\psi \in \mathcal{S}(\mathbb{R})$ whose Fourier transform
$\hat{\psi}$ is supported in $\{\xi : 1 \leq |\xi| \leq 2\}$ and satisfies
$\sum_{j \in \mathbb{Z}} |\hat{\psi}(2^{-j}\xi)|^2 = 1$ for $\xi \neq 0$.

The **Littlewood‚ÄďPaley projector** at dyadic scale $j$ is

$$P_j f(x,t) := \int_{\mathbb{R}} \hat{f}(\xi,t)\, \hat{\psi}(2^{-j}\xi)\, e^{2\pi i x\xi}\, d\xi .$$

$P_j f$ is the piece of $f$ with spatial frequency $|\xi| \sim 2^j$.

### 1.2 Spatial localisation window

Fix a non-negative Schwartz bump $\varphi \in \mathcal{S}(\mathbb{R})$ with
$\int \varphi = 1$.  For $x_0 \in \mathbb{R}$ and scale $k \in \mathbb{N}$ define

$$\varphi_{x_0,k}(x) := 2^k\, \varphi\!\bigl(2^k(x - x_0)\bigr) ,$$

which has unit integral and is concentrated in $|x - x_0| \lesssim 2^{-k}$.

### 1.3 Canonical envelope (Definition)

> **Definition (c_{j,x‚āÄ,k}).** For $f : \mathbb{R} \times \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ (space √ó time), $j,k \in \mathbb{N}$, $x_0 \in \mathbb{R}$, and $t \in \mathbb{R}$, define
>
> $$c_{j,x_0,k}(t)^2 \;:=\; \int_{\mathbb{R}} \bigl|P_j f(x,t)\bigr|^2\, \varphi_{x_0,k}(x)\, dx .$$

This is the **localized $L^2$ norm** of the $j$-th Littlewood‚ÄďPaley piece of $f$,
weighted by the spatial window $\varphi_{x_0,k}$ centred at $x_0$ at scale $2^{-k}$.

**Properties:**
- $c_{j,x_0,k}(t) \geq 0$ for all $j,k,x_0,t$.
- By Plancherel: $\int_{\mathbb{R}} c_{j,x_0,k}(t)^2\, dx_0 = \|P_j f(\cdot,t)\|_{L^2}^2$.
- The mapping $x_0 \mapsto c_{j,x_0,k}(t)^2$ is a smooth function that recovers the
  frequency energy of $f$ at scale $j$ in a window of size $2^{-k}$ around $x_0$.

## 2. The Near-Diagonal Summability Hypothesis H_FE

> **Hypothesis H_FE.** There exist constants $A > 0$ and $J \in \mathbb{N}$ (the
> *near-diagonal bandwidth*) such that for every spatial scale $k \in \mathbb{N}$,
>
> $$2^k \int_{\mathbb{R}} \sum_{j=k}^{k+J} 2^{j/2}\, c_{j}(t)^2\; dt \;\leq\; A .$$

Here we write $c_j(t)$ for the $L^2$-in-$x_0$ average of $c_{j,x_0,k}$; see ¬ß3.

**Interpretation.** H_FE says that the total (time-integrated) energy living in the
near-diagonal frequency band $[2^k, 2^{k+J+1})$, weighted by the dyadic factor
$2^{j/2}$ (which appears in the Navier‚ÄďStokes energy flux), is bounded uniformly
in the scale $k$.  This is the key bound that prevents a nonlinear cascade from
concentrating energy along the diagonal of frequency space.

## 3. Bootstrap Reduction

### 3.1 Bootstrap hypothesis (Hyp_Bootstrap)

> **Hyp_Bootstrap.** There exist $C > 0$ and $\alpha > 3/2$ such that
>
> $$\int_0^T c_j(t)^2\, dt \;\leq\; C \cdot 2^{-\alpha j} \qquad \forall j \in \mathbb{N}.$$

This is a *pointwise-in-j* decay condition on the time-integrated scale energies.

### 3.2 Reduction theorem

> **Theorem (H_FE_of_bootstrap).** If Hyp_Bootstrap holds with parameters $(C, \alpha)$
> then H_FE holds with bound $A = C(J+1)$.

**Proof.**  For each $k$:

$$2^k \sum_{j=k}^{k+J} 2^{j/2}\, \int c_j^2
  \;\leq\; C \cdot 2^k \sum_{j=k}^{k+J} 2^{j/2} \cdot 2^{-\alpha j}
  \;=\; C \cdot 2^k \sum_{j=k}^{k+J} 2^{j(1/2 - \alpha)} .$$

Since $\alpha > 1/2$, the map $j \mapsto 2^{j(1/2-\alpha)}$ is decreasing, so
each term in the sum is at most the $j=k$ term:

$$\leq C \cdot (J+1) \cdot 2^k \cdot 2^{k(1/2-\alpha)}
  = C(J+1) \cdot 2^{k(3/2 - \alpha)} .$$

Since $\alpha > 3/2$ we have $3/2 - \alpha < 0$, hence $2^{k(3/2-\alpha)} \leq 1$
for all $k \geq 0$.  Therefore

$$2^k \sum_{j=k}^{k+J} 2^{j/2} \int c_j^2 \;\leq\; C(J+1) =: A . \qquad \square$$

The theorem is stated and proved in `lean/FreqEnvelopes.lean` as

## 4. Worked Example

**Geometric-decay envelope.** Let $\varphi \in L^2(\mathbb{R})$, $\varphi \geq 0$,
and $r \in (0, 2^{-3/2})$.  Define

$$c_j(t) := r^j\, \varphi(t) .$$

Then:

$$\int c_j(t)^2\, dt = r^{2j}\, \|\varphi\|_{L^2}^2 = C \cdot 2^{-\alpha j}$$

where $C = \|\varphi\|_{L^2}^2$ and $\alpha = -\log_2(r^2) = -2\log_2(r) > 3$
(since $r < 2^{-3/2}$ implies $\log_2 r < -3/2$ so $\alpha = -2\log_2 r > 3$).
Note that $\alpha > 3 > 3/2$, so the bootstrap requirement $\alpha > 3/2$ is
strictly satisfied (with room to spare).

Hyp_Bootstrap is satisfied, and H_FE holds with $A = C(J+1) = \|\varphi\|_{L^2}^2 (J+1)$.

This example is formalized as `TOGT.FreqEnvelopes.geometricEnvelope` and
`TOGT.FreqEnvelopes.geometricEnvelope_bootstrap`.
